## 1945–1970

Timeline:
* 1945–49
  - Chinese Civil War (East Asia)
    - Kuomintang/Republic of China vs Chinese Communist Party; civil-revolutionary war. Resumed after Japan’s surrender and ended with communist conquest of mainland China.
    - Outcome: the PRC was founded in 1949 and the ROC retreated to Taiwan. 
    - Impact: it created the China-Taiwan divide and radically shifted the Cold War balance in Asia. 

* 1945–49
  - Indonesian National Revolution (Southeast Asia)
    - Indonesian republicans vs the Netherlands; anti-colonial war of independence. Fighting followed Indonesia’s 1945 declaration of independence and ended with Dutch recognition of Indonesian sovereignty in 1949. 
    - Impact: it became a major model for postwar Asian decolonization. 

* 1946–54
  - First Indochina War (Southeast Asia)
    - France vs the Viet Minh; anti-colonial war and Cold War precursor. It ended after the French defeat at Dien Bien Phu and the Geneva Accords of 1954. 
    - Impact: French colonial rule ended and the division of Vietnam set the stage for the Vietnam War. 

* 1947–49
  - First Kashmir War / First India-Pakistan War (South Asia)
    - India vs Pakistan, with local militias in Kashmir; interstate war rooted in partition and accession disputes.
    - Outcome: a UN-backed ceasefire froze the conflict and left Kashmir divided. 
    - Impact: it created the enduring India-Pakistan territorial dispute. 

* 1948–49
  - First Arab-Israeli War (Middle East)
    - Israel vs Egypt, Transjordan/Jordan, Iraq, Syria, and Lebanon; interstate war following the end of the British Mandate and Arab rejection of partition.
    - Outcome: Israel survived and expanded beyond the UN partition boundaries; armistice lines were established in 1949. 
    - Impact: it began the long Arab-Israeli conflict and the Palestinian refugee crisis. 

* 1948–60
  - Malayan Emergency (Southeast Asia)
    - British/Commonwealth and Malayan authorities vs communist insurgents; counterinsurgency and decolonization war.
    - Outcome: the insurgency was defeated and the emergency ended in 1960. 
    - Impact: it became one of the most cited Cold War counterinsurgency cases. 

* 1950–53
  - Korean War (East Asia)
    - North Korea and later China vs South Korea, the United States, and the UN Command; interstate war with civil-war roots.
    - Outcome: armistice, not peace, with the peninsula still divided. 
    - Impact: it militarized the Cold War in Asia and entrenched the Korean partition. 

* 1954–62
  - Algerian War (North Africa)
    - France vs the FLN; anti-colonial war of independence.
    - Outcome: Algerian independence. 
    - Impact: it accelerated French decolonization and reshaped French domestic politics. 

* 1956
  - Suez Crisis (Middle East)
    - Egypt vs Britain, France, and Israel; interstate war and imperial crisis.
    - Outcome: Egypt emerged politically strengthened under intense U.S. and Soviet pressure on the invaders to withdraw. 
    - Impact: it marked the decline of British and French imperial influence in the Middle East. 

* 1960–65
  - Congo Crisis (Central Africa)
    - competing Congolese factions, secessionists, Belgian intervention, and UN forces; post-independence civil war, secession war, and intervention crisis.
    - Outcome: the Congolese state survived but remained unstable. 
    - Impact: it became one of the defining early UN peacekeeping and Cold War crises in Africa. 

* 1955–75
  - Vietnam War (Southeast Asia)
    - North Vietnam and the Viet Cong vs South Vietnam and its allies, chiefly the United States; civil, interstate, and proxy war.
    - Outcome: North Vietnamese victory and the fall of Saigon in 1975, followed by reunification. 
    - Impact: it discredited U.S. interventionism for a generation and became a central case in modern war studies. 

* 1960–96
  - Guatemalan Civil War (Central America)
    - Guatemalan state vs leftist guerrillas; civil war and Cold War counterinsurgency.
    - Outcome: peace accords in 1996. 
    - Impact: it left a deep legacy of atrocities, displacement, and militarized politics in Central America. 

* 1967
  - Six-Day War (Middle East)
    - Israel vs Egypt, Syria, and Jordan; short interstate war.
    - Outcome: decisive Israeli victory and seizure of Sinai, Gaza, the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and the Golan Heights. 
    - Impact: it transformed the strategic map of the Middle East and made occupation and territorial status central to later diplomacy and war. 

* 1967–70
  - Nigerian Civil War / Biafran War (West Africa)
    - Nigeria vs secessionist Biafra; civil and secession war.
    - Outcome: Biafra surrendered and Nigeria was re-unified. 
    - Impact: the war became a landmark humanitarian crisis, especially because of famine and blockade imagery. 

* 1971
  - Bangladesh Liberation War and 1971 India-Pakistan War (South Asia)
    - Bengali nationalists and India vs Pakistan; secession war fused with interstate war.
    - Outcome: Pakistani surrender in East Pakistan and the creation of Bangladesh. 
    - Impact: it remade South Asia’s political map and deepened India-Pakistan hostility. 

* 1973
  - Yom Kippur War (Middle East)
    - Egypt and Syria vs Israel; interstate war aimed at reversing the losses of 1967.
    - Outcome: initial Arab gains were reversed, but the war broke the image of Israeli invulnerability and opened the path to later diplomacy, especially Egyptian-Israeli peace. 
    - Impact: it triggered a major oil and strategic shock worldwide. 

## 1975–1999

Timeline:
* 1975–90
  - Lebanese Civil War (Middle East)
    - a shifting mix of Lebanese militias, the PLO, Syria, Israel, and later Hezbollah; multi-sided civil and proxy war.
    - Outcome: formal end in 1990, but with enduring militia and sectarian structures. 
    - Impact: it fragmented the Lebanese state and made Lebanon a central arena of regional proxy conflict. 

* 1975–2002
  - Angolan Civil War (Southern Africa)
    - MPLA vs UNITA and earlier FNLA, with major Cuban, South African, U.S., and Soviet involvement; civil and proxy war.
    - Outcome: MPLA victory after decades of war. 
    - Impact: it was one of the longest and most internationalized African Cold War conflicts. 

* 1977–78
  - Ogaden War (Horn of Africa)
    - Somalia vs Ethiopia; interstate territorial war.
    - Outcome: Ethiopian victory with Soviet and Cuban support, followed by Somali withdrawal. 
    - Impact: it destabilized Somalia and helped set conditions for later Somali state collapse. 

* 1978–89
  - Soviet-Afghan War (South/Central Asia)
    - Soviet Union and Afghan communist government vs mujahideen; occupation, counterinsurgency, and proxy war.
    - Outcome: Soviet withdrawal; the communist regime survived briefly but eventually fell. 
    - Impact: it weakened the USSR, internationalized jihad networks, and destabilized Afghanistan for decades. 

* 1980–88
  - Iran-Iraq War (Middle East)
    - Iran vs Iraq; prolonged interstate war.
    - Outcome: bloody stalemate ending in a ceasefire in 1988. 
    - Impact: it devastated both states, normalized large-scale missile and chemical warfare in the region, and helped set the stage for Iraq’s 1990 move against Kuwait. 

* 1982
  - Falklands War (South Atlantic)
    - Argentina vs the United Kingdom; short interstate war over the Falkland/Malvinas Islands.
    - Outcome: British victory and reoccupation of the islands. 
    - Impact: it strengthened Thatcher politically and accelerated the collapse of Argentina’s military junta. 

* 1983–2005
  - Second Sudanese Civil War (Northeast Africa)
    - Sudanese government vs SPLA/M and related southern forces; civil war with strong religious, ethnic, and regional dimensions.
    - Outcome: the 2005 Comprehensive Peace Agreement and, eventually, South Sudan’s independence in 2011. 
    - Impact: it was one of the deadliest African wars of the late 20th century and directly led to partition. 

* 1990–91
  - Gulf War (Middle East)
    - Iraq vs a U.S.-led UN-backed coalition; interstate coalition war after Iraq’s invasion of Kuwait.
    - Outcome: Kuwait was liberated and Iraqi forces were expelled. 
    - Impact: it marked the high point of post-Cold War coalition warfare and inaugurated the long Iraq-containment era. 

* 1991–2001
  - Yugoslav Wars (Balkans / Southeastern Europe)
    - successor republics, Serb-led Yugoslav forces, local militias, and later NATO; secession, civil, and ethnic wars.
    - Outcome: Yugoslavia broke apart; Bosnia ended at Dayton; Kosovo ended with NATO intervention and Serbian withdrawal. 
    - Impact: they reintroduced ethnic cleansing and mass atrocity to Europe and reshaped post-Cold War intervention doctrine. 

* 1990–94
  - Rwandan Civil War and 1994 Genocide (Central/East Africa)
    - Rwandan government vs the RPF, followed by genocidal mass killing by Hutu extremists; civil war and genocide.
    - Outcome: RPF victory and end of the genocide. 
    - Impact: it killed hundreds of thousands, generated massive refugee flows into eastern Zaire/Congo, and fed directly into the Congo wars. 

* 1996–97
  - First Congo War (Central Africa)
    - Laurent Kabila and allied forces backed by Rwanda/Uganda vs Mobutu’s Zaire; rebellion, regime-change war, and regional intervention.
    - Outcome: Mobutu fell and Zaire became the Democratic Republic of the Congo. 
    - Impact: it opened the door to the far larger Second Congo War. 

* 1998–2003
  - Second Congo War (Central Africa)
    - DRC government and allied states vs multiple rebel forces backed by neighboring states; civil, interstate, and proxy war.
    - Outcome: formal war end in 2003, but eastern Congo remained violent. 
    - Impact: it became the deadliest war since World War II and is often called “Africa’s World War.” 

* 1998–2000
  - Eritrea-Ethiopia War (Horn of Africa)
    - Eritrea vs Ethiopia; interstate border war.
    - Outcome: ceasefire and the Algiers Agreement. 
    - Impact: despite its limited territorial issue, it caused major casualties, militarized both states, and poisoned regional politics for years. 

## 21st century

Timeline:
* 2001–21
  - Afghanistan War (South/Central Asia)
    - United States, NATO, and the Afghan government vs the Taliban and allied insurgents; intervention, occupation, and insurgency war.
    - Outcome: the Taliban were overthrown in 2001 but returned to power in 2021 after the U.S. withdrawal. 
    - Impact: it became the longest U.S. war and a defining case of state-building failure under insurgent pressure. 

* 2003–present
  - Darfur Conflict (Sudan / Sahel edge)
    - Sudanese government and Janjaweed-linked militias vs Darfur rebel groups; civil war, mass atrocity, and counterinsurgency.
    - Outcome: no clean final settlement; the conflict merged into Sudan’s wider crises. 
    - Impact: it became one of the most prominent atrocity crises of the early 21st century and shaped debates on genocide and responsibility to protect. 

* 2003–11
  - Iraq War (Middle East)
    - U.S.-led coalition vs Iraq, followed by insurgent and sectarian warfare; invasion, occupation, and civil-conflict phase.
    - Outcome: Saddam Hussein’s regime fell quickly, but the occupation produced a long insurgency and deep state fragility. 
    - Impact: it destabilized Iraq, damaged U.S. credibility, and helped create the conditions in which ISIS later rose. 

* 2008
  - Russo-Georgian War (Europe/Eurasia)
    - Russia and South Ossetian/Abkhaz forces vs Georgia; short interstate war.
    - Outcome: Russian military victory and a French-brokered ceasefire. 
    - Impact: it signaled the return of overt Russian force in the post-Soviet space and foreshadowed later wars in Ukraine. 

* 2011–20
  - Libyan Civil War(s) (North Africa)
    - anti-Qaddafi rebels vs Qaddafi in 2011, followed by rival Libyan authorities and militias after 2014; uprising, intervention, and civil war.
    - Outcome: Qaddafi fell in 2011, but Libya fragmented and a later ceasefire froze rather than fully solved the conflict. 
    - Impact: Libya became a durable hub of militia rule, foreign intervention, and Mediterranean insecurity. 

* 2011–24, with instability continuing
  - Syrian Civil War (Middle East)
    - Assad regime vs a wide array of rebel groups, jihadist movements, Kurdish-led forces, and multiple foreign interveners; civil, proxy, and transnational war.
    - Outcome: Assad regained much of the country with Russian and Iranian support but was then removed from power in December 2024; instability persists. 
    - Impact: the war shattered Syria, produced vast displacement, and transformed the regional balance. 

* 2013–18, with renewed instability afterward
  - South Sudan Civil War (East Africa)
    - forces loyal to Salva Kiir vs forces aligned with Riek Machar; civil war with strong elite and ethnic dimensions.
    - Outcome: a 2018 peace agreement reduced but did not eliminate violence. 
    - Impact: it devastated the world’s newest state and showed how quickly secession can give way to internal collapse. 

* 2014–present
  - Yemeni Civil War (Arabian Peninsula / Red Sea)
    - Houthi movement vs the Yemeni government and, from 2015, a Saudi- and UAE-led coalition; civil and regional proxy war.
    - Outcome: no comprehensive peace; a truce from 2022 reduced front-line fighting, but the conflict remains active and spills into Red Sea security. 
    - Impact: it produced one of the world’s worst humanitarian crises and linked Yemen to wider regional escalation. 

* 2014–19, with insurgent remnants afterward
  - War against ISIS (Iraq and Syria)
    - ISIS vs Iraqi, Syrian, Kurdish, and international coalition forces; transnational jihadist war and counteroffensive.
    - Outcome: ISIS lost its territorial “caliphate” by 2017–19 but remained active as an insurgent network. 
    - Impact: it redefined counterterrorism, urban warfare, drone use, and coalition operations in the Middle East. 

* 2014–present
  - Russia-Ukraine War (Europe/Eurasia)
    - Russia and its proxies vs Ukraine; interstate war that began with Crimea and Donbas and expanded into full-scale invasion in February 2022.
    - Outcome: ongoing as of April 6, 2026. 
    - Impact: it is the largest interstate war in Europe since 1945 and has transformed NATO, sanctions policy, arms production, and drone-centered warfare. 

* 2020–22
  - Tigray War (Horn of Africa)
    - Ethiopian federal government and allies vs the TPLF/Tigrayan forces; civil war with regional spillover.
    - Outcome: the Pretoria agreement ended large-scale fighting. 
    - Impact: the war caused massive death, displacement, and famine risk, and it exposed how fragile Ethiopia’s federal settlement had become. 

* 2021–present
  - Myanmar Civil War (Southeast Asia)
    - military junta vs the National Unity Government, People’s Defence Forces, and ethnic armed organizations; civil war following the 2021 coup.
    - Outcome: ongoing as of April 6, 2026. 
    - Impact: it has hollowed out central authority, intensified regional instability, and turned Myanmar into one of the world’s most complex multi-actor wars. 

* 2023–present
  - Sudan Civil War (Northeast Africa)
    - Sudanese Armed Forces vs the Rapid Support Forces; civil war and state-fragmentation struggle.
    - Outcome: ongoing as of April 6, 2026. 
    - Impact: it has become one of the gravest humanitarian catastrophes in the world and threatens prolonged partition-like fragmentation. 

* 2023–present
  - Israel-Hamas War / Gaza War (Middle East)
    - Israel vs Hamas and other Palestinian militants in Gaza; interstate-adjacent territorial war with broader regional spillover.
    - Outcome: ongoing as of April 6, 2026. 
    - Impact: it has devastated Gaza, intensified the wider Arab-Israeli conflict, and triggered linked crises in Lebanon, the Red Sea, and regional diplomacy. 

## Main conclusions

1. The first post-1945 era was dominated by decolonization and partition wars. Indonesia, Indochina, Algeria, Palestine/Israel, and Kashmir show that the end of empire produced many of the world’s most enduring conflict lines. 

2. The Cold War did not suppress war; it redirected it. Korea, Vietnam, Angola, Afghanistan, the Arab-Israeli wars, and Central American conflicts show that great-power rivalry was usually fought through regional proxies, intervention, and client states rather than direct U.S.-Soviet war. 

3. The post-1991 era did not become peaceful. It shifted toward state breakdown, ethnic war, and intervention politics, especially in Yugoslavia, Rwanda, Congo, Iraq, Libya, Syria, Yemen, and Sudan. 

4. Since 2014, major interstate war has clearly returned. Ukraine, the continuing Arab-Israeli wars, and current Middle Eastern escalations show that the older idea that large interstate war was largely obsolete no longer holds. 

5. Across the whole period, the biggest pattern is not “war disappeared,” but “the dominant form of war changed”: from decolonization, to proxy war, to state-fragmentation war, and now to a world in which high-intensity interstate war and multi-actor civil war coexist. 


6. “Post-1945 peace” is misleading. Great-power war was avoided, but large interstate, colonial, civil, and proxy wars remained constant. 

7. Many “civil wars” were also international wars in practice, because neighboring states or great powers intervened directly. Angola, Afghanistan, Syria, Yemen, Congo, and Libya are central examples. 

8. Formal end dates can mislead. A treaty, armistice, or ceasefire often ended one phase while violence, insurgency, or state collapse continued. Korea, Sudan, Iraq, Libya, Syria, Yemen, and Congo all fit this pattern.
